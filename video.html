Video Production Project

Welcome to my Video Production Project!

In this project I compiled images, audio, text, and formatting in order to create a pursuading video for why you should visit Minnesota.

This video is about Minnesota and outlines a few cool things you can do there on vacation. I wrote my script first so I knew what I wanted to say in my video. After I wrote the script, I practiced it a couple times to make sure it sounded good. Then, I recorded the audio on my phone using a voice recorder app.

Then, I browsed the internet for images that matched key words in my script. I highlighted my script with the parts where a new image would appear and then chose images for each part.

This is where I then opened Video Editor and began putting the different parts together. I started by uploading all of the images into Video Editor and arranging them so that they were in order. Next, I uploaded the voice recording over the images.

Next, I edited the duration of each image in the video to match up with the voice recording. This is where I spent the bulk of my time on this project. This took a lot precision work to get it right down to the millisecond so that the image appeared as long as it was supposed to. I started at the beginning and just worked my way through the entire video editing the length of each image.

Then, I went through and animated the video so that it wasn't just images and audio. This is when I added a subtle background music. I ended up using an audio called "On Hold" which I found in the Video Editor app. I lowered the volume so that it was very subtle and wasn't distracting.

Next, I looked at each image to decide how I wanted to animate them. Some of the images I added text over to connect the audio to a visual text element. This is when I also added motion for how the images appeared. I also messed around with 3D elements that I found in the Video Editor app. I added a snowfall 3D visual to the images which contained snow. I also added a glowing sun 3D visual to the image of the State Fair.

Finally, I uploaded the video to Youtube!
